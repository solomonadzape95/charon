How judges score — and what to optimize for
30%
Agentic sophistication
AI must make real decisions — not just automate. Pricing logic, value evaluation, routing between providers all count. "AI-flavored automation" scores poorly.
30%
Traction
Actual humans using it. Test USDC flowing. Judges want to see creators earning and readers paying — not just a working demo on localhost.
20%
Circle tool usage
Use Wallets, Gateway/Nanopayments, App Kit, Contracts, x402, USDC creatively. Don't just bolt on one endpoint — compose the stack.
20%
Innovation
Novel approaches and emergent behavior. New territory beats polished re-runs. The citation-toll and streaming-pay ideas are fresh ground.
Key insight: Agency and traction are equally weighted at 30% each — and traction is the one most teams neglect. Getting 5–10 real users in 3 weeks beats having a beautiful demo with zero usage. Ship on day 3, get users by day 7.
How to structure your 3 weeks to win
Pick one idea from column A + elements from column B
Column A (traction): Scrobble sidecar or Ghost pay-per-article — both have built-in audiences. Column B (agency + innovation): Add an AI agent that dynamically prices content, evaluates whether a citation is worth paying for, or routes payments across providers. Stack them: a Ghost plugin + an AI pricing agent that sets per-article price based on content length, author reputation, and current demand.
Week 1 — Ship the core, get first users
Get a working payment flow live by day 3. Deploy on testnet. Post in the Canteen Discord and self-hosted communities (r/selfhosted, Navidrome forums, Ghost forum). First users are your traction evidence. You cannot get traction without something live.
Week 2 — Add the AI layer, make it genuinely agentic
This is where you separate from other teams. Add real AI decision-making: dynamic pricing, budget management, cost-vs-value evaluation. Use Circle's Agent Stack. The AI should be making hundreds of decisions per hour — pricing each play, each article, each citation individually.
Week 3 — Traction push + submission polish
Submit early on day 14 so you can resubmit with better numbers. The form asks "how many users do you have" — make sure the answer isn't zero. A 3-minute Loom showing real payments flowing to a real creator is worth 10x a polished slide deck.
The combo that's hardest to beat: Scrobble sidecar (traction from self-hosted music community, 8+ compatible servers) + AI pricing agent (sets per-listen rate dynamically by artist popularity, time-of-day demand, listener's monthly budget) + x402 + Arc Gateway + MusicBrainz MBID registry. Hits RFB 6, Prior Art #5, all four judging criteria.
